### 2. Today's Highlights

#### OpenAI Announces End of Video Generation AI "Sora"
OpenAI has decided to discontinue "Sora," the video generation AI app released in Fall 2025 that garnered significant attention. According to an announcement on its official social media, the app and its developer API will be phased out. This decision stems from persistent concerns from Hollywood stakeholders and creators regarding copyright, as well as criticism over the proliferation of "AI slop" (valueless AI-generated content). In particular, the issue of deepfake videos of prominent individuals was a major blow. Partners such as Disney have also issued statements respecting this decision, and it is believed that OpenAI will temporarily withdraw from the video generation market to rebuild its product strategy. This move suggests a transition from the rapid expansion phase of generative AI to a phase that demands more responsible operation and sustainable business models.

Source: [Sora Official SNS](https://twitter.com/soraofficialapp/status/1770000000000000000)

#### NVIDIA Donates GPU Management Technology to Kubernetes Community
At KubeCon Europe, NVIDIA announced its donation of its "Dynamic Resource Allocation (DRA) Driver" for GPUs to the Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF). This will enable more open and transparent management and orchestration of GPU resources on Kubernetes, which serves as the foundation for AI workloads. By placing technology that was previously managed solely by NVIDIA under community control, increased efficiency in building AI infrastructure across the industry is expected. Furthermore, NVIDIA's "KAI Scheduler" has been adopted as a CNCF sandbox project, accelerating efforts to optimize inference for large-scale AI models. This demonstrates the company's strategic intent to enhance its dominance not only in hardware but also in the software ecosystem within the AI competition.

Source: [NVIDIA Official Newsroom](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/about/news/nvidia-donates-dynamic-resource-allocation-driver-for-gpus-to-kubernetes-community/)
