#### Life Sciences & AI Drug Discovery
Insilico Medicine announced a comprehensive research and licensing agreement with pharmaceutical giant Eli Lilly for AI-driven drug discovery. This partnership is valued at up to approximately $2.75 billion. Eli Lilly will leverage Insilico's Pharma.AI platform to accelerate the creation of innovative oral drugs across multiple therapeutic areas. Dr. Alex Zhavoronkov, CEO of Insilico, emphasized that AI technology has scaled from biomarkers to life models, enabling the simultaneous identification of targets common to multiple diseases. This marks a critical turning point where AI-native drug discovery is being integrated into the core of pharmaceutical giants' R&D pipelines, with expectations for reduced development timelines and improved success rates.

#### Space Engineering & Science
NASA announced a fundamental review of its "Artemis" lunar exploration program strategy. The construction of the "Gateway" lunar orbital station, previously a priority, will be temporarily paused. New priorities include the construction of a lunar base, sustained human presence on the Moon, and acceleration of nuclear propulsion technology. This shift aims to establish American leadership in deep space exploration beyond Earth's low orbit. NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman stated the goal is to standardize lunar activities and utilize commercially reusable systems, aiming for manned lunar landings every six months in the future. This move suggests a shift in focus towards more practical ground-based exploration amidst intensifying great power competition in space development.

#### Management & Organizational Theory
As AI adoption rapidly progresses, the nature of organizational transformation is being questioned. According to an AACSB research report, simply measuring the frequency or duration of AI use is insufficient; for organizations to truly achieve results, the "quality of behavior" in how employees utilize AI must be evaluated. Furthermore, reports from Deloitte and others point to the possibility of "culture debt" arising from AI as it redefines existing workflows and roles. To foster a culture where AI and humans coexist, clear delegation of decision-making authority and responsibility is needed, along with seamless organizational designs where AI complements human judgment.

Source: [AACSB](https://aacsb.edu/insights/articles/2026/march/research-roundup-march-2026)

#### Robotics & Autonomous Agents
Medtronic announced that its "Stealth AXiS" surgical navigation and robotic system has received FDA 510(k) clearance for cranial and ENT surgeries. This system integrates surgical planning, navigation, and robotics, aiming to improve the surgeon's workflow. With this clearance, surgeons can create detailed, patient-specific brain maps, enabling more precise minimally invasive procedures. The automation of medicine through robotics is evolving from simple equipment deployment to tools that augment surgeon skills by integrating advanced visual processing and navigation.
