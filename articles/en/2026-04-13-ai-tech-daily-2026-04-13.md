### 2. Today's Highlights

#### Anthropic Releases Frontier Model "Claude Mythos" Specialized for Cybersecurity
Anthropic has released a preview of its new model, "Claude Mythos," which boasts the company's highest coding and vulnerability detection capabilities to date. This is provided on a limited basis to major tech companies and security expert organizations as part of a new initiative called "Project Glasswing." Claude Mythos is capable of identifying complex vulnerabilities that have previously eluded human discovery, holding the potential to dramatically enhance software security. To protect this powerful tool from misuse, Anthropic has implemented stringent access controls and is offering free usage credits to over 40 organizations that maintain cloud, OS, and major browser infrastructure. This is a critical and innovative effort to fortify defenses in a landscape where AI's offensive capabilities are increasingly surpassing human defensive abilities.

#### Meta Announces Next-Generation Personal AI "Muse Spark"
Meta has announced a new model called "Muse Spark." This is Meta's latest and most powerful model, developed using a stack rebuilt from the ground up by Meta Superintelligence Labs. Muse Spark enhances user experience personalization significantly with an "Instant" mode for everyday tasks and a "Thinking" mode for complex problems requiring deeper logical reasoning. It is currently available on the Meta AI app and website and is scheduled for rollout to Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, and smart glasses within the coming weeks. The company has also hinted at the future introduction of multimodal capabilities and a "Contemplating" mode for longer-duration reasoning, aiming for a transition from mere chatbots to true "personal superintelligence."
