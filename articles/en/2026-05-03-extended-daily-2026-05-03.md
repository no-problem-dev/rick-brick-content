#### Life Sciences & AI Drug Discovery
Insilico Medicine, an AI-driven drug discovery startup, has announced that its novel TNIK inhibitor 'Rentosertib (ISM001-055),' designed by its 'Pharma.AI' platform, has received IND (Investigational New Drug) approval from Chinese regulators for an inhaled formulation. This candidate drug, which completed target discovery to molecular design in just 18 months using AI, is drawing attention as a practical Proof of Concept for AI drug discovery in lung diseases.

#### Psychology & Cognitive Science
A research team at the University of Alabama at Birmingham, funded by the NIH, has revealed through a large-scale study that specialized cognitive training to enhance the speed and accuracy of visual information processing can reduce the risk of developing Alzheimer's disease and related dementias in older adults by up to 25%. The findings, published in 'Alzheimer's & Dementia,' suggest that 'speed training,' which promotes implicit learning, may contribute to long-term cognitive function maintenance more than memory training.

Source: [University of Alabama at Birmingham](https://www.uab.edu/news/research/item/15535-study-shows-brain-training-can-reduce-alzheimer-s-and-dementia-risk-by-25-percent)
