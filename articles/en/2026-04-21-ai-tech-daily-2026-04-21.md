### 2. Today's Highlights

#### OpenAI Accelerates the Cyber Defense Ecosystem
To enhance defensive capabilities in cybersecurity, OpenAI has expanded its "Trusted Access for Cyber (TAC)" program, offering limited access to GPT-5.4-Cyber to hundreds of security teams. This model is tuned to identify vulnerabilities across entire codebases and provide insights for defenders to implement fixes rapidly.

The background to this initiative is the evolution of AI-powered attack methods by adversaries, exacerbating the "speed gap" with defenders. OpenAI emphasizes that creating an environment where defenders can safely benefit from AI is crucial for protecting society's digital infrastructure. Technically, the model's inference capabilities are optimized for defensive code analysis, while incorporating strict guardrails to limit offensive exploit generation. Looking ahead, the company plans to promote standardization of defense technologies through collaboration with entities like CAISI (Center for AI Safety Innovation).
[Source: OpenAI Official Blog "Accelerating the cyber defense ecosystem that protects us all"](https://www.openai.com/index/accelerating-the-cyber-defense-ecosystem-that-protects-us-all/)

#### Evolution of "Agents SDK" Standardizes Agent Development
OpenAI has announced the next generation of its "Agents SDK," designed to enable developers to build and manage autonomous AI agents more efficiently. The core of this update is the introduction of a "model-native harness" and a "native sandbox execution" environment, allowing models to directly manipulate files, execute code, and track long-term tasks.

While previous agent development tended to rely heavily on prompt engineering, this update provides state management, deterministic guardrails, and secure interaction with the environment at a foundational level. This accelerates the transition from mere chatbots to agents capable of autonomously performing enterprise business processes such as data analysis, code editing, and system monitoring. This signifies a shift in AI development from "prompt-based instructions" to "agent architecture design," allowing developers to focus on building domain-specific logic.
[Source: OpenAI Official Blog "The next evolution of the Agents SDK"](https://www.openai.com/index/the-next-evolution-of-the-agents-sdk/)
