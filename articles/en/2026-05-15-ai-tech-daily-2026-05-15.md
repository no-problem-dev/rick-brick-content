#### Google DeepMind Announces "AI Pointer" for Intuitive Operation in the AI Era
Google DeepMind has unveiled an experimental technology called "Reimagining the mouse pointer for the AI era," which allows AI to understand visual and semantic context on a PC screen and assist user operations. This technology enables users to point at text, images, or datasets on the screen with their mouse and combine it with voice commands to instantly perform tasks such as "summarize this graph" or "show routes to this address." While traditional AI assistants operated within separate windows, this pointer technology allows users to interact with AI on any element of the screen without leaving their existing application environment. This move redefines the concept of a "pointer" on digital devices, a concept that has remained unchanged for half a century, and is expected to be integrated into platforms like Chrome as part of the future Gemini experience. The vision of AI providing a seamless operational experience without interrupting the user's workflow is predicted to have a significant impact on future UI design.
[Source: Google DeepMind Official Blog](https://deepmind.google/technologies/gemini/pro/)

#### OpenAI Proposes Establishment of International AI Governance Organization
Ahead of the meeting between the US President and the Chinese President, Chris Lehane, a senior official at OpenAI, expressed support for establishing an international governance body to ensure AI safety. This organization, modeled after the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) that monitors nuclear non-proliferation, aims to have countries including the US and China join to formulate global safety standards. The company views AI as a dual-use technology with risks for both defense and offense, even as the cybersecurity capabilities of its latest models advance. In relation to this, OpenAI is implementing a strategy to deepen dialogue with government agencies by granting access to its latest models to entities like the European Commission, thereby promoting transparent development and responsible AI implementation. Amidst escalating international competition for AI leadership, this attempt by a private AI company to lead international regulatory harmonization could be a precursor to future technology diplomacy.
