### 2. Today's Highlights

#### NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Nano Omni: A New Standard for Efficient Agent AI
NVIDIA has announced a new model, "Nemotron 3 Nano Omni," designed to dramatically improve the inference efficiency of agent systems. This model is a multimodal model capable of processing visual, auditory, and language inputs within a single loop. Previously, agents combined separate model stacks for image processing, audio analysis, and language reasoning, leading to inference delays and complexity. Nemotron 3 Nano Omni employs a 30B-A3B hybrid Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture, activating only the necessary experts to minimize inference costs while maintaining high accuracy. The model demonstrates top-tier performance on leaderboards for document understanding and video/audio comprehension, and is designed to simultaneously achieve improved throughput and cost reduction, especially in large-scale agent workflows. The introduction of this model marks a critical turning point for the industry, facilitating a "departure from fragmented stacks" in building practical autonomous agents.

Source: [NVIDIA Blog "NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Nano Omni Powers Multimodal Agent Reasoning in a Single Efficient Open Model"](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/nemotron-3-nano-omni-multimodal-agent/)

#### OpenAI Launches Models, Codex, and Managed Agents on AWS
OpenAI has announced the availability of its latest models, its agent-focused application "Codex," and "Managed Agents" through Amazon Web Services (AWS). This partnership enables companies to seamlessly leverage OpenAI's inference capabilities on AWS's robust infrastructure. In particular, the AWS offering of Codex's model integration and Managed Agents functionality will significantly accelerate agent development that integrates with proprietary enterprise knowledge and data. This integration broadens options for securely deploying OpenAI's technology in enterprise environments with stringent security and compliance requirements, creating an environment where developers can focus on building Agentic AI workflows without concerns about infrastructure management. Industry analysts view this move as "further commoditization of AI models and deepening competition at the infrastructure layer."

Source: [OpenAI Blog "OpenAI models, Codex, and Managed Agents come to AWS"](https://openai.com/index/openai-models-codex-and-managed-agents-come-to-aws/)
