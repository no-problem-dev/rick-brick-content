#### Anthropic Launches Frontier Model "Claude Mythos" and "Project Glasswing"

Anthropic announced its new frontier model, "Claude Mythos Preview," possessing extremely high cyber attack capabilities. This model has reached a level that surpasses human performance in autonomously identifying software vulnerabilities and generating exploits. For instance, it autonomously discovered and verified a 27-year-old vulnerability in OpenBSD and a 16-year-old flaw in FFmpeg that had remained unaddressed for years. Considering the risk of misuse due to its "extreme" capabilities, Anthropic made the groundbreaking decision not to release this model to the public.

As an alternative, Anthropic has launched "Project Glasswing." This initiative involves over 40 major partners, including Amazon Web Services (AWS), Apple, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and CrowdStrike, to use Claude Mythos exclusively for "defense" purposes. Anthropic is offering up to $100 million in usage credits to promote this defensive utilization and will also donate $4 million to open-source security organizations. This move sets a new standard for how companies should manage their frontier models as "defense infrastructure" amidst a landscape where the advancement of AI models dramatically shifts the balance between offense and defense. It is anticipated that automated vulnerability diagnosis and remediation by AI will become commonplace in the security industry within the next few years.

Source: [Anthropic Official "Project Glasswing"](https://www.anthropic.com/news/project-glasswing)

#### NVIDIA Unveils World's First Quantum AI Models "NVIDIA Ising"

NVIDIA announced "NVIDIA Ising," the world's first open-source family of quantum AI models designed to dramatically improve "calibration" and "Quantum Error Correction," major challenges in quantum computing. The biggest bottlenecks in current quantum hardware are extremely high error rates and unstable calibration processes required to maintain large quantum circuits. The Ising models optimize these processes using AI, achieving up to 2.5x faster error correction decoding and 3x higher accuracy compared to traditional mathematical methods.

These models integrate seamlessly with NVIDIA's "CUDA-Q" software platform and "NVQLink" hardware interconnect, enabling real-time control in hybrid quantum-classical computing environments. Leading research institutions such as Academia Sinica, Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory, and Harvard University have already announced their adoption. NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang stated, "AI will become the 'control plane' for making quantum computing practical," and this release proves that AI is key to breaking through the physical limitations of next-generation computer science. This could potentially accelerate the commercialization roadmap for quantum technology by several years.

Source: [NVIDIA Official News "NVIDIA Launches Ising"](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-launches-ising-open-ai-models)
