### 2. Today's Highlights

#### Anthropic Publishes "What 81,000 People Want from AI"

Anthropic released the results of a large-scale qualitative study revealing users' expectations, concerns, and actual usage patterns around AI. Described as "the largest and most multilingual qualitative survey to date," the study collected approximately 81,000 responses and comprehensively analyzed how AI users around the world are using AI, what they expect from it, and what worries them.

The results show that the majority of users prioritize "accuracy" and "reliability" above all else — valuing factual correctness over creativity or speed. Privacy concerns remain high, and non-English-speaking users in particular expressed a strong desire for improved AI performance in their native languages. Anthropic indicated it will use these findings to enhance Constitutional AI and strengthen multilingual model capabilities.

Surveys of this scale are rare in the AI industry, and this one will serve as an important benchmark for user-centered AI development.

Source: [Anthropic "What 81,000 People Want from AI"](https://www.anthropic.com/news/81k-interviews)

#### Google DeepMind Announces Cognitive Framework for Measuring AGI Progress

Google DeepMind published a cognitive-science-based framework for systematically measuring progress toward AGI. Until now, definitions of AGI and corresponding measurement criteria have varied widely among researchers, making objective evaluation of progress difficult. The new framework proposes decomposing human cognitive abilities into multiple dimensions — including reasoning, planning, learning, perception, and social cognition — and assessing AI systems' capabilities along each dimension in stages.

Particularly noteworthy is the shift from a single benchmark score to a multidimensional cognitive profile for evaluating AI capabilities. This enables more nuanced assessments such as "this model is at human-level performance for reasoning, but still at an early stage for social cognition." DeepMind has made the framework publicly available and is calling for its adoption as an industry standard.

Source: [Google DeepMind "Measuring Progress Toward AGI: A Cognitive Framework"](https://deepmind.google/discover/blog/measuring-progress-toward-agi-a-cognitive-framework/)

#### AlphaGo at 10: A Technical Legacy Spanning from Games to Biology

Google DeepMind published a blog post reflecting on the 10 years since AlphaGo defeated the world Go champion. AlphaGo's victory over Lee Sedol in 2016 was a landmark milestone that demonstrated the practical power of deep reinforcement learning to the world, and its influence has extended far beyond the world of games.

AlphaGo's technical foundations directly inspired subsequent DeepMind projects including AlphaFold (protein structure prediction), GNoME (new materials discovery), and AlphaGeometry (mathematical olympiad problem solving). AlphaFold in particular has predicted more than 200 million protein structures, bringing revolutionary change to drug discovery and biological research. The post also highlights how members of the original AlphaGo team are now contributing to their respective projects — a rare example of a single research project continuing to generate ripple effects a decade later.

Source: [Google DeepMind "From Games to Biology and Beyond: 10 Years of AlphaGo's Impact"](https://deepmind.google/blog/10-years-of-alphago/)
