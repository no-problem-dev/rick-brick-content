### 2. Today's Highlights

#### US Government Announces Comprehensive "National AI Legislative Framework"
**Summary**: On March 20, 2026, the US Trump administration announced the "National AI Legislative Framework" aimed at balancing the development of AI technology, maintaining national competitiveness, and addressing public concerns. The framework presents six guiding principles to Congress to promote industrial innovation while responding to societal challenges posed by AI.

**Background**: Concerns about a "patchwork" of different AI regulations being enacted at the state level have been prevalent, with the industry calling for unified federal rules. The administration views this as a "hindrance to AI innovation" and recommends developing federal legislation that preempts state laws to some extent.

**Technical Explanation**: This framework is based on a "light touch" approach to AI development, emphasizing fair use interpretations of copyright, AI's contribution to national security, and workforce retraining, rather than strict restrictions on specific technologies. It particularly aims to streamline the AI infrastructure permitting process and expand access to development environments.

**Impact and Outlook**: Future US AI policy will be debated in Congress based on this framework. While the industry generally welcomes it, citizen groups concerned about privacy protection and the societal risks of large-scale AI have criticized it as "too biased towards corporate protection." Intense discussions are expected during the subsequent legislative process.

**Source**: [White House Official Announcement](https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2026/03/20/president-donald-j-trump-unveils-national-ai-legislative-framework/)

#### Meta Launches Global AI-Powered Content Moderation
**Summary**: Meta has begun the global rollout of AI-powered support assistants and content moderation on Facebook and Instagram. This will automate tasks such as password resets, privacy settings, reporting inappropriate content, and appealing content removal decisions.

**Background**: Meta has long faced limitations with human content moderation. Specifically, the surge in scams, fake celebrity profiles, and inappropriate sexual content overwhelmed existing staffing levels.

**Technical Explanation**: The AI system being deployed demonstrated success in initial experiments, detecting and blocking 5,000 daily scam attempts that human moderators had missed. The AI can also recognize patterns of anomalous behavior, such as "an account being accessed from a new location, a password change, and profile edits," which might appear harmless individually to a human, thereby preventing account takeovers.

**Impact and Outlook**: This move solidifies the shift towards AI moderation across the entire technology industry. Meta plans to transition more operations to AI over the next few years, excluding certain "high-impact" decisions such as complex judgments or reporting to law enforcement. This is expected to reduce operational costs and provide faster feedback to users.

**Source**: [Meta Official Newsroom](https://about.fb.com/news/2026/03/meta-ai-support-assistant-global-rollout/)

### 4. Summary and Outlook
The clear trend emerging from today's news is the "agentification" of AI and its "safe operation in real-world applications." Major companies like Meta, Microsoft, and NVIDIA have determined that agents have reached a stage where they can achieve human-level productivity, and are now focusing on monitoring, moderation, and security frameworks to ensure their reliability and safety. The AI industry's focus will likely shift entirely from mere performance competition to a governance phase focused on how to manage the "responsibility" for tasks autonomously executed by agents.
