#### US Government Announces Comprehensive "National AI Legislative Framework"
**Summary**: On March 20, 2026, the US Trump administration announced its "National AI Legislative Framework" aimed at balancing the advancement of AI technology, maintaining national competitiveness, and addressing public concerns. The framework presents six guiding principles to Congress to foster industrial innovation while responding to societal challenges posed by AI.

**Background**: Concerns have been raised about a "patchwork" of AI regulations varying by state, and the industry has called for unified federal rules. The administration views this as a "hindrance to AI innovation" and recommends federal legislation that preempts some state laws.

**Technical Explanation**: This framework is based on "light touch" intervention in AI development, emphasizing fair use interpretations of copyright, AI's contribution to national security, and workforce retraining, rather than strict limitations on specific technologies. It particularly aims to streamline AI infrastructure permitting processes and expand access to development environments.

**Impact and Outlook**: Future US AI policy will be debated in Congress based on this framework. While the industry has largely welcomed it, civil society groups concerned about privacy protection and the societal risks of large-scale AI have criticized it as "too biased towards corporate protection." Heated discussions are expected during the legislative process.

#### Meta Launches AI-Powered Content Moderation Globally
**Summary**: Meta has begun the global rollout of AI-powered support assistants and content moderation for Facebook and Instagram. This will automate tasks such as password resets, privacy settings, reporting inappropriate content, and appealing content removal decisions.

**Background**: Meta has long faced the limitations of human content moderation. The surge in scams, fake celebrity profiles, and inappropriate sexual content, in particular, has overwhelmed traditional staffing levels.

**Technical Explanation**: The AI system deployed in this initiative detected and blocked 5,000 scam attempts per day in initial experiments that human moderators had overlooked. The AI also successfully prevented account takeovers by recognizing patterns such as "an account accessed from a new location, a password change, and profile edits," which might appear "harmless" if judged individually by a human.

**Impact and Outlook**: This move solidifies the shift towards AI moderation across the technology industry. Meta plans to transition more operations to AI over the next few years, excluding some "high-impact" decisions like complex judgments and reporting to law enforcement. This is expected to reduce operational costs and provide faster feedback to users.
