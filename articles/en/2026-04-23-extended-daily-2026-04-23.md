### 2. Sector-Specific News

#### Economics / Financial Engineering
In Taiwan, the "Financial Sector Sovereign AI Infrastructure" project has been launched to strengthen the technological foundation of the financial industry. This project, with strong support from the National Science and Technology Council (NSTC) and the Digital Development Department (MODA), is being jointly built by local banks and financial institutions. The objective is to move away from excessive reliance on foreign models such as OpenAI's ChatGPT and Google's Gemini, and to establish "sovereign AI" that deeply incorporates Taiwan's unique financial regulations, supervisory practices, and industry-specific expertise. This infrastructure also aims to promote financial inclusion and provide information to educational organizations in the future.

#### Life Sciences / Drug Discovery AI
Significant advancements have been made in cell imaging technology. A research team from the Salk Institute and Albert Einstein College of Medicine has developed a new technique, "VIS-Fbs (Visible Spectrum Antigen-Stabilized Fluorescence Nanobodies)" and published their findings in Nature Methods. This technique utilizes nanobodies, which are extremely small protein fragments, to label target proteins with high specificity. Compared to conventional methods, it dramatically reduces background noise, enabling real-time, high-precision visualization of individual molecule dynamics even in complex environments such as living brain tissue and zebrafish embryos. This represents a groundbreaking tool for tracking signaling pathways in tumor growth and observing viral infections.

#### Life Sciences / Drug Discovery AI / Business Administration
Curve Biosciences has announced computational and clinical advancements in "Whole-Body Intelligence" for chronic disease monitoring. This technology utilizes the company's "Whole-Body Atlas" to analyze organ-specific biological signals obtained from blood using AI. Particularly in diseases such as liver cirrhosis, it holds the potential to detect signs of disease progression more accurately and at an earlier stage than previously possible with conventional ultrasound or blood protein tests. For clinicians, the establishment of non-invasive monitoring methods is expected to significantly contribute to improving patient treatment adherence.
